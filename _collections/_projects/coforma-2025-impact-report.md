<h1 class="mt-2 mb-2">Building an Annual Impact Reporting Practice</h1>

<p class="mt-2 mb-4">
Coforma needed a stronger way to communicate its impact to partners, prospective clients, employees, recruits, and peers across the civic technology and digital services space. I helped build and evolve an annual impact reporting practice across two reporting cycles, moving the work from a static year-end update into a multi-format storytelling system.
</p>

<p class="mt-2 mb-4">
The final approach included a public web report, accessible PDF, internal and external video, social campaign, email distribution, and performance reporting. Rather than treating the report as a one-time deliverable, we built a repeatable process for gathering stories, validating metrics, involving stakeholders, and distributing the work across multiple channels.
</p>

<h2 class="mt-2 mb-2">Core Problem</h2>
<p class="mt-2 mb-4">
Coforma had meaningful work to share, but that impact was distributed across projects, teams, and subject matter experts. Without a repeatable reporting practice, it was difficult to gather stories, validate metrics, align stakeholders, and present the work in a way that felt specific, credible, and human.
</p>

<p class="mt-2 mb-4">
The central challenge was not simply producing another annual report. It was building a durable storytelling practice that could help the organization show its work clearly to current and potential partners, prospective employees, and peers across the digital services ecosystem.
</p>

<h2 class="mt-2 mb-2">Strategic Approach</h2>
<p class="mt-2 mb-4">
The strategy began with a central question: how do we show Coforma’s impact in a way that feels specific, credible, and human?
</p>

<p class="mt-2 mb-4">
I helped evaluate peer and competitor impact reports across civic technology, digital services, philanthropy, and mission-driven consulting. I reviewed examples for structure, format, narrative approach, visual treatment, metrics, distribution, and opportunities to differentiate.
</p>

<p class="mt-2 mb-4">
That research shaped several decisions:
</p>
<ul>
    <li>The report needed to be story-forward, not just metrics-heavy.</li>
    <li>The experience needed to work across website, accessible PDF, social, email, and video.</li>
    <li>The storytelling needed to foreground people impacted by the work, not only internal accomplishments.</li>
    <li>The report needed to support multiple audiences, including partners, prospective partners, candidates, and industry peers.</li>
    <li>The process needed to become repeatable so future reports could be easier to produce and richer in substance.</li>
</ul>

<h2 class="mt-2 mb-2">Solution / Execution</h2>
<p class="mt-2 mb-4">
I helped lead the strategy, production, publishing, and distribution of Coforma’s annual impact reporting practice across two reporting cycles. My responsibilities included market and competitor research, content strategy, story and metrics planning, stakeholder engagement, executive and subject matter expert interviews, video scripting and production, website build, social publishing, distribution planning, performance reporting, and workflow improvement.
</p>

<p class="mt-2 mb-4">
For the 2025 report, I produced a major video component that included perspectives from approximately half a dozen members of leadership, along with input from additional staff and subject matter experts. I wrote the script, identified interview subjects, coordinated and recorded interviews via Zoom and Riverside.fm, filmed b-roll in person, sourced supporting assets, and shaped many perspectives into a cohesive narrative.
</p>

<h2 class="mt-2 mb-2">Results & Impact</h2>
<p class="mt-2 mb-4">
The project created a clearer annual rhythm for communicating Coforma’s work and value. The 2024 report established the structure, and the 2025 report expanded it into a richer multimedia experience with stronger stakeholder input and more intentional distribution.
</p>
